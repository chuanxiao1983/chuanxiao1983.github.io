Biography

I am an associate professor with the Big Data Engineering Laboratory at Osaka University and a guest associate professor with the Database Laboratory at Nagoya University. I received my Ph.D. from the University of New South Wales in 2010 under the supervision of Xuemin Lin and Wei Wang.

Research

Selected current topics and representative publications.

Smart Agent-Based Modeling (SABM)

Using foundation-model agents (LLMs/VLMs) to formulate simulations in natural language and study phenomena across economics and behavioral science.

  • LLM-Based Social Simulations Require a Boundary
  • Seven Security Challenges in Cross-domain Multi-agent LLM Systems
  • Shall We Team Up: Spontaneous Cooperation of Competing LLM Agents (EMNLP 2024 Findings) · Code
  • Smart Agent‑Based Modeling · Slides

Data Lake Management & Data Preprocessing

Leveraging LLMs for table understanding, data cleaning, integration, and augmentation in large data lakes.

  • On the Use of LLMs for Table Tasks (CIKM 2024 Tutorial)
  • Jellyfish: A Large Language Model for Data Preprocessing (EMNLP 2024) · Models & Dataset
  • Large Language Models as Data Preprocessors (TaDA 2024)
  • BClean: A Bayesian Data Cleaning System (ICDE 2024)

Similarity Query Processing

Efficient methods for high‑dimensional ANN/MIPS, sets, strings, and generic similarity operations.

  • Probabilistic Routing for Graph‑based ANNS (ICML 2024)
  • Pigeonring: Faster Thresholded Similarity Search (PVLDB 2018)
  • Monotonic Cardinality Estimation of Similarity Selection (SIGMOD 2020)
  • High‑Dimensional Similarity Query Processing for Data Science (KDD 2021 Tutorial)

Data Science for Digital Humanities

Computational approaches in sociology, law, education, and history through data‑centric methods.

  • Legal Fact Prediction (EMNLP 2025)
  • Information entropy in programming education (ToE)
